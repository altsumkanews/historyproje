Independence and Evolution of Conflict (1991–2022)

Shtan Bohdan квітень 29, 2026

 

In 1991, Ukraine proclaimed independence. However, for Moscow, this was seen as a "temporary misunderstanding."

  • The Yeltsin Quote: "Let them play around, we'll squeeze them in our fist later anyway."

  • Budapest Memorandum (1994): Ukraine gave up the world's third-largest nuclear arsenal for security guarantees that Russia later violated.

  • Tuzla Island Conflict (2003): Russia's first open attempt to test Ukraine's borders.

    2014 & 2022: Open Aggression

    Following the Revolution of Dignity, Russia occupied Crimea and started the war in Donbas. The 2022 full-scale invasion was an attempt at a "final solution to the Ukrainian question." Putin’s speeches deny Ukraine's agency, echoing the 1863 Valuev Circular.

    Summary: The conflict is an existential war. Russia follows a cycle: Declarative recognition → Hybrid pressure/denial of identity → Military terror and annexation. Today, 117,000 square km (20% of Ukraine) is occupied—an area equal to four Belgiums or nearly two Czech Republics.
