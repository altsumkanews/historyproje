Ukrainian Revolution (1917–1921): Conflict of Statehoods

Shtan Bohdan 29/04/2026

 

The collapse of the Russian Empire in 1917 created a power vacuum. In Ukraine, this led to the creation of the Ukrainian People's Republic (UNR), headed by the Central Rada (Parliament).

  • IV Universal of the Central Rada (1918): "Відтепер Українська Народна Республіка є незалежною, суверенною, вільною країною українського народу"
    "From now on, the Ukrainian People's Republic becomes an independent, subordinated to no one, free, sovereign state of the Ukrainian people."

While the Ukrainian project envisioned democratic institutions, Bolshevik Russia offered a centralized socialist model. In December 1917, the Bolsheviks created an alternative government in Kharkiv and launched a military campaign against the UNR. This shows that the conflict was both national and ideological. Ultimately, Ukraine lost its independence, but its proclamation became an important historical precedent.
